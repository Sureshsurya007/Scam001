Karnataka Govt Launches ₹50,000 Free Housing Grant for All Families Below Poverty Line — Here's How to Apply

The state cabinet has approved an emergency housing scheme under the Rajiv Gandhi Awas Yojana extension, with funds to be disbursed directly to eligible families before July 15, 2026.
By Staff Reporter, Karnataka Today Jun 21, 2026  |  6:45 PM IST
🏠 ₹50,000 Housing Grant
for BPL Families
Chief Minister chairing the emergency cabinet meeting on the new housing scheme. (Representational image)

In a major relief announcement for millions of Karnataka residents, the state government on Sunday approved a ₹50,000 direct housing grant for all families registered under the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category. The decision was taken during an emergency cabinet session presided over by the Chief Minister and is expected to benefit over 32 lakh families across the state.

"Every family in Karnataka deserves a roof over their head. This grant is our commitment to making that a reality before the end of this financial year."

The scheme will be implemented through the Rajiv Gandhi Awas Yojana extension framework, with the state contributing ₹35,000 and the central government contributing the remaining ₹15,000. Funds will be transferred directly to the beneficiary's Aadhaar-linked bank account within 30 working days of approval.

Who is Eligible?

Families registered under the BPL list of 2011 census, SECC-2011 data, or those holding a valid Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) or Priority Household (PHH) ration card are eligible. The applicant must be a Karnataka resident for at least five years and must not own any pucca house in any part of India.

Applications can be submitted online through the Karnataka Seva Sindhu portal or at the nearest Gram Panchayat or BBMP ward office. Officials have confirmed that a dedicated helpline — 1800-425-0820 — has been activated for queries and will remain operational 24/7 until the application window closes on July 10, 2026.

Documents Required

Applicants will need to submit their Aadhaar card, BPL/ration card, bank passbook, recent passport-sized photograph, and a self-declaration of not owning any pucca house. No fee will be charged at any stage of the application process, and officials have warned citizens against touts or middlemen demanding money.

The Housing Department Secretary confirmed that a dedicated monitoring cell will review all applications within 15 days to prevent duplication and fraud. District Collectors have been instructed to set up special camps in rural areas to assist applicants who face difficulties with the online portal.

Opposition leaders welcomed the scheme but demanded clarity on funding timelines, pointing to previous housing schemes where disbursements were delayed by over a year. The government, however, maintained that funds have already been allocated in the revised state budget and that no delays are anticipated.

This announcement comes ahead of the upcoming local body elections and is seen as a significant political move by the ruling party. Political analysts say the scheme, if implemented swiftly, could have a considerable impact on voter sentiment in rural districts where housing remains a pressing concern.
